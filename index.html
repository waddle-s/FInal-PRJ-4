Education + Design Background

High School

Before coming to the University of Waterloo, I attended KCI—a high school in Kitchener. I took graphic design courses in grades 10 and 11, where I was introduced to Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop. I can credit my interest for design to hese classes. It became obvious to me then that I wanted to pursue something creative in my post-secondary studies.

One of my favourite design projects was the very first one I did. We were tasked with designing a logo. Essentially, we had complete creative liberty, and could make our logo about anything we wanted, which I thought was a good approach for an introductory project. I ended up creating a rat head logo for an (imaginary, unfortunately named) streetwear brand I dubbed "TRATZ"

Why GBDA?

The Perfect Mix

GBDA is the ideal hybrid of design, business, and technology. During my univerity application process, GBDA immediately stood out to me. There is no other program that offers students this intersection of skill. This program offers its students a comprehensive range of creative and business endeavours to help each individual pave their own distinctive path to a career that fits their passions and abilities. As someone who had no idea what they wanted to pursue after high school (and is still admittedly unsure), I am confident GBDA will give me the tools I need to be successful in a wide variety of fields.

Writing

I actually enjoy writing 10-page essays; I recognize I am probably in the minority here. I understand that there is a general consensus among students that writing for academic purposes is monotonous and annoying. While I don't necessarily disagree, I find it relaxing— it can be a nice repreive from other genres of work. I also love to write creatively, though I don't do it as much as I'd like to.

French

I've been a french immersion kid most of my life. Learning a new language is very fulfilling, and lots of fun. I earned my DELF certification (level B2) in my last year of highschool, and hope to one day speak fluently. University has put a bit of a pause on my development, but I don't intend to abandon the skills I've spent over a decade cultivating— I'm just on a minor hiatus.
